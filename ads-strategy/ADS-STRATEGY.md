## Why Your Ads Failed (Honest Diagnosis)

Before fixing anything, understand what almost certainly went wrong:

| Root Cause | What It Looks Like | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| No conversion tracking | You spent money but had no idea what worked | Install Meta Pixel + Google Tag properly FIRST |
| Wrong audience | Targeting too broad, or the wrong people entirely | Narrow to SMB owners in UK trades |
| Ad sent to homepage | Homepage doesn't convert cold traffic | Build/use a dedicated landing page |
| Ad copy was generic | "We build great websites" — everyone says this | Lead with pain, proof, and specificity |
| Budget too spread thin | £5/day on 3 platforms = nothing learns | Concentrate budget on 1–2 platforms only |
| No funnel | Expecting cold traffic to buy immediately | Awareness → Consideration → Conversion flow |

## Platform Selection

For a web design agency targeting UK trades (plumbers, electricians, builders, roofers, landscapers):

| Platform | Priority | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Meta (Facebook/Instagram)** | **PRIMARY** | Trades SMB owners are heavy Facebook users. Visual portfolio works perfectly. Cheapest CPL for your niche. |
| **Google Search** | **SECONDARY** | Captures high-intent searches: "website for plumber UK", "web design trades". These people are ready to buy. |
| LinkedIn | Skip for now | Too expensive for targeting SMB tradespeople. They're not on LinkedIn for business decisions. |
| TikTok | Later (Month 3+) | Could work for organic but ad budget too thin to test now. |

**Start with Meta only if budget is under £1,500/mo. Add Google Search at £1,500+/mo.**

## Your Unfair Advantage: 5 Testimonials

You have social proof. This is more valuable than any clever ad copy. Most beginners waste it — here is how to use it properly:

### Testimonial Hierarchy (Use in this order)
1. **Video testimonials** — film a 30-second clip of a client saying what problem you solved and the result. This is your #1 creative priority.
2. **Before/After website screenshots** — trade's old website → new Stackwell website side by side.
3. **Quote cards** — pull the best line from each testimonial, put it on a clean graphic with their name, trade, and location.
4. **Case study style** — "Dave's Plumbing got 3 new enquiries in the first week of their new site."

### Where Testimonials Go in Your Funnel
```
Cold traffic → Brand awareness ad (what you do, who it's for)
         ↓
Retargeting → Testimonial/social proof ad ("See what [trade] businesses say")
         ↓
Hot retargeting → Offer ad ("Free website audit" or "Get a quote")
```

**The #1 mistake: showing testimonial ads to cold traffic who don't know you yet. Warm them up first.**

## Tracking Setup (Do This Before Spending a Penny)

This is why your first ads failed. Without tracking, you are flying blind.

### Priority Order
| Step | Platform | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Meta | Install Meta Pixel on your website. Verify with Pixel Helper Chrome extension. |
| 2 | Meta | Set up "Lead" or "Contact" conversion event (fires when someone submits your contact form) |
| 3 | Google | Install Google Tag Manager on your site |
| 4 | Google | Set up Google Analytics 4 + import goals |
| 5 | Google | Install Google Ads conversion tag (fires on thank-you page) |
| 6 | UTMs | Add UTM parameters to every ad URL: `?utm_source=meta&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign=leads` |

**If you cannot install tracking: use Meta Lead Forms instead of sending traffic to your website. Lead forms collect name/email/phone inside Meta — no pixel needed. Start here.**

## The 3x Kill Rule (When to Stop an Ad)

Apply this ruthlessly. Most beginners let bad ads run forever:

```
PAUSE immediately if:
- Spent 3× your target CPL with 0 conversions
- CTR is below 0.8% after 1,000 impressions (creative is being ignored)
- Frequency hits 4+ on a cold audience (they've seen it too many times)

DO NOT restart without making a change (new creative, new copy, new audience)
```

## Offer Strategy (What to Offer in Your Ads)

The biggest mistake is asking cold traffic to "buy now." Use a low-friction offer:

| Funnel Stage | Offer | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Cold (awareness) | "Free website audit for UK trades" | Gives value, no commitment |
| Warm (retargeting) | "See our portfolio — trades websites from £X" | Anchors price, shows proof |
| Hot (decision) | "Book a free 15-min call" | Easy yes, gets them in your pipeline |

**Avoid "Contact Us" as your CTA. Too generic. "Book a free call" or "Get your free audit" converts 2-3× better.**

## 12-Week Implementation Roadmap

### Week 1–2: Foundation (Do Not Skip)
- [ ] Install Meta Pixel + verify it fires on form submissions
- [ ] Install Google Tag Manager
- [ ] Set up GA4 + conversion tracking
- [ ] Build Meta Lead Form as backup (in case pixel takes time)
- [ ] Upload email list to Meta as Custom Audience
- [ ] Create Lookalike Audience from email list (1%)
- [ ] Audit your landing page: mobile speed, clear CTA, social proof visible

### Week 3–4: Launch Meta Only
- [ ] Launch Awareness campaign (£5–£10/day, Reach objective)
- [ ] Launch Leads campaign (£15–£25/day, Lead Gen objective)
- [ ] Launch Retargeting campaign (£5/day, website visitors + video viewers)
- [ ] Run all 3 creatives in the Leads campaign — split test
- [ ] Monitor daily for spend pacing and tracking fires

### Week 5–8: Optimise
- [ ] Kill worst-performing creative (3x Kill Rule)
- [ ] Double budget on winning creative
- [ ] Make 2 new creatives based on what's working (testimonial videos if possible)
- [ ] Review search terms if Google is live — add negatives
- [ ] Check landing page CVR (should be 5%+; if lower, fix page first)

### Week 9–12: Scale
- [ ] Launch Google Search (if budget allows)
- [ ] Keywords: "web design [trade] UK", "website for plumber", "trade website design"
- [ ] Increase Meta budget by 20% max per week on winners
- [ ] Introduce Before/After creative format
- [ ] Begin building case study content from best client results
